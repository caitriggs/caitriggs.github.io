Day Two — Saturday, May 9

Dresses, Scrubs & Galbi

Dress shopping, Korean spa with Amanda, dinner with the crew in KTown.

Morning
Formal Dress Shopping
Head to DTLA or mid-city for formal event dress shopping (~30 min from Santa Monica). Call ahead to confirm Saturday hours and whether appointments are needed.
Dress Shop Options
CoutureShopLA — DTLA Lili Bridals LA Fashion District (Santee St)

CoutureShopLA (532 E 11th St) is the top pick — specializes in evening gowns, red carpet dresses, and formal event wear. Staff is highly reviewed for helping find the right fit. The Fashion District on Santee Street (9th–14th) is great if you want to browse many shops in one walk.

Afternoon
Korean Spa Day — Amanda Time
The main event of the afternoon. Budget 3–4 hours for the full experience — soak, scrub, sauna, and decompress. Both top picks are in Koreatown, ~25 min from Santa Monica.
Option 1 — Wellness Retreat Vibes
Olympic Spa

Women-only spa that combines the best of a luxury day spa with traditional Korean wellness. Intimate, uncrowded (appointment or membership required). Features a halotherapy salt room, Japanese charcoal room, jade healing lounge, outdoor veranda with complimentary teas and snacks. Body scrub treatments are among the best in the city. No jjimjilbang or restaurant — this is pure, quiet spa immersion.

TypeWomen-only
VibeLuxury / intimate / quiet
BookCall ahead — required
Option 2 — The Full Korean Mega-Spa Experience
Wi Spa

The granddaddy of KTown spas — four floors spanning women's and men's wet rooms, a co-ed jjimjilbang with heated specialty rooms, a rooftop terrace, full Korean restaurant, and a kids zone. Open 24/7, $40 admission. You and Amanda do the gender-separated tubs, scrubs, and saunas, then meet up in the communal jjimjilbang upstairs. The scale is impressive and the rooftop is a nice touch.

Vibes caveat: Wi Spa is bigger, louder, and busier — especially on Saturdays. TVs are everywhere, including in the saunas, often blasting news or Korean dramas. It's more communal hangout than tranquil retreat. If you want spectacle and the iconic KTown mega-spa experience, this is it. If you want to actually decompress, Olympic is the move.

TypeCo-ed (separated wet rooms)
VibeMassive / social / bustling
BookWalk-in, 24/7
Alternative
Spa Palace

Co-ed option if Max and James want in. Open 24/7, Himalayan salt room, excellent scrubs, good on-site restaurant. Large jjimjilbang with mostly muted TVs — calmer than Wi Spa. 48-hour cancellation policy for booked treatments.

Evening
Dinner in Koreatown — All Four
Reunite with Max and James for a memorable KTown dinner. Book a reservation now — Saturday nights fill up fast.
Dinner Recommendations
Quarters — KBBQ, MICHELIN Guide Park's BBQ — the gold standard Chosun Galbee — stunning patio Guelaguetza — Oaxacan The Prince — iconic bar

Quarters — MICHELIN Guide featured. Quarter-pound portions let you sample a wide range of cuts (galbi, pork jowl, Wagyu brisket). Full cocktail bar, cheese fondue side, and a lively group-friendly energy. Perfect format for 4 people who want to try everything.

Park's BBQ — The purist pick. USDA Prime and Kobe-style beef, widely considered the best KBBQ in LA. The $70 galbi short rib explains itself in one bite. Valet available.

Chosun Galbee — The most atmospherically memorable option. Granite tabletops, koi ponds, and a huge outdoor patio that feels like a rainforest. Upscale KBBQ.

Book on Resy or OpenTable. Saturday KTown KBBQ waits can hit 60–90 min at peak (7–9 PM) — reserve or arrive early.

Late Night
Back to Santa Monica
~25 min drive back to the Sandbourne. Or extend the night with karaoke in KTown — it's an unspoken rule of a night out in Koreatown that you may end up in a private noraebang room.

Day Three — Sunday, May 10

Antiquities to Comedy

Getty Villa, Pacific brunch, hotel swap, and the main event.

9:30 AM
Check Out — Sandbourne
Pack up, bags in the car. You're heading north on PCH — the Getty Villa is only 15 minutes up the coast from Santa Monica.
10:00 AM – 12:30 PM
Getty Villa
A recreation of an ancient Roman country home housing Greek, Roman, and Etruscan antiquities dating back to 6,500 BC. The gardens alone are worth the trip. Currently showing The Book of the Dead exhibition through November 2026.
Must Know

Admission is free but timed-entry reservation is required — you cannot walk in without one. Book at getty.edu as soon as possible. Sunday slots fill up weeks ahead. Parking is $20 (credit card only). Plan 2–3 hours. The Villa entrance is accessible from northbound PCH only.

Hours10 AM – 5 PM (closed Tuesdays)
Parking$20 before 3 PM
Duration2–3 hours
