Tuesday, June 16

Tuesday — Into the Wild Green

Morning
Ferry to Port Townsend

Leave from downtown — Populus is just a 5-minute walk from Colman Dock. Catch the ferry to Bainbridge Island (~35 min crossing), then drive to Port Townsend (~1 hr). About 2.5 hours total.

Ferry tip: No reservations on this route. Arrive 30-45 min before your target sailing. Summer schedule starts June 14. Grab Molly Moon's ice cream at the Colman Dock terminal while you wait.
Late Morning
Summit Picnic Lunch — Mount Walker

A short detour off US-101 just south of Quilcene. Drive 4.25 miles up the gravel summit road (FR 2730) to the summit.

Lunch at the summit: fire up the camp stove and grill gyro wraps with premade tzatziki and tabbouleh. Picnic with a view before rolling into Port Townsend.

Dogs welcome on leash at both viewpoints. The summit road is gravel but well-maintained — fine for any vehicle. About 15 min from US-101 to the top.

1.5 miles from downtown. Miles of trails, beach walks, old military bunkers to explore, and Point Wilson Lighthouse. Dogs on leash. Great for wearing out the pups.

Chetzemoka Park

Quiet waterfront park with grassy lawns, gardens, and beach access. Named after a S'Klallam chief. There's an off-leash dog park inside the park too, so Lucy, Mustang, and Lola can run.

Thursday — To Whidbey Island

Morning
Farewell Port Townsend

Late Morning
Ferry to Whidbey
⛴

Port Townsend → Coupeville (Keystone)

~35 minute crossing. Reservations available and highly recommended for mid-June. Book at wsdot.wa.gov/ferries. Arrive 30 min before sailing.

Midday
Coupeville & Price Sculpture Forest

The ferry drops you right in the heart of Whidbey. Explore historic Front Street in Coupeville, grab Penn Cove mussels at a local spot, and stop by Whidbey Pies.

Price Sculpture Forest
Price Sculpture Forest
678 Parker Rd, Coupeville · Free · sculptureforest.org
  • 16-acre outdoor sculpture park in a century-old forest
  • Two trail loops with 27+ sculptures hidden among the trees
  • Free self-guided audio tour on your phone (no app needed)
  • Walkable from downtown Coupeville (1.5 mi path) or short drive
  • Open daily 8am to sunset or 7pm
  • Amazing photo ops with sculptures, moss, and old-growth canopy
No dogs allowed at the Sculpture Forest. Lucy, Mustang, and Lola can hang in the car while everyone explores. It's a quick visit.

Friday, June 19

Friday — Whidbey Adventure

Morning
Ebey's Landing Bluff Trail
Ebey's Landing Bluff Trail

The signature Whidbey hike — 3.5 to 5.2 miles depending on your route. Coastal bluffs 200+ feet above Puget Sound with views of the Olympic Mountains, Port Townsend across the water, and Mt. Rainier on a clear day. Dogs welcome on leash.

Trailhead tip: Start from Prairie Overlook (Sunnyside Cemetery) for the full experience. Discover Pass required. Check tides if doing the beach loop — avoid high tide. ~15 min drive from Captain Whidbey.
Afternoon
Pick Your Adventure

🐕 For the Dogs

Double Bluff Beach — 2-mile off-leash beach, Mt. Rainier views (~35 min south). Or Greenbank Farm — 3 miles off-leash trails plus pie, cheese, and wine shops (~20 min south). Lucy, Mustang & Lola paradise.

🥂 For the Humans

Spoiled Dog Winery — dog-friendly outdoor tasting. Or explore Langley — charming village with boutiques and the Saltwater Fish House (pet-friendly patio). Kayak from Captain Whidbey's dock.

Evening
Last Night on the Island

Dinner at Captain Whidbey restaurant or drive into Coupeville. S'mores at the lodge fire pit — staff-tended on Friday evenings.

Saturday, June 20

Saturday — Homeward

Morning
Deception Pass State Park
Deception Pass Bridge

Check out of Captain Whidbey and drive north on SR-20 (~25 min). Deception Pass is on the way home. Discover Pass required ($30/year or $10/day). Dogs allowed on leash throughout.

Deception Pass Bridge

Walk across the iconic bridge, 180 ft above swirling waters. Sidewalks on both sides with sweeping views.

Sand Dunes Trail

0.8-mile paved interpretive loop at West Beach. 800+ year old Douglas fir, observation deck, Rosario Strait views. Note: ~150 ft of NE corner washed out as of Jan 2026.

Route home: Cross Deception Pass Bridge north to Fidalgo Island, then hop on I-5 south from Burlington/Mt. Vernon. About 1.5 to 2 hours to West Seattle depending on traffic. No ferries needed.
